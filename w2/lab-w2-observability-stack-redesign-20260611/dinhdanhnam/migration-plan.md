# Eight-Week Migration Plan

## Principles

- No business-hours observability blackout: Datadog, Splunk, and PagerDuty remain active until each replacement has passed its gate.
- Dual-run before every cutover: Datadog remains authoritative for APM/metrics, Splunk remains authoritative for log/audit search, and PagerDuty remains authoritative for paging until the corresponding new path has passed its gate.
- Rollback target: every cutover must be reversible inside 30 minutes by restoring the previous exporter, alert route, or dashboard link.

## Week-By-Week Plan

| Week | Work | Go / no-go gate | Rollback path |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Stand up non-production Grafana, VictoriaMetrics, Loki, Tempo, Alloy, Alertmanager, and IRM sandbox. Import service topology from `services.json`. Define approved metric labels and log fields. | Platform team can query sample metrics, logs, and traces for all 10 services in Grafana; no production alerts depend on the new stack. | Tear down sandbox and keep all production telemetry on Datadog, Splunk, PagerDuty. |
| 2 | Deploy Alloy/OTel side-by-side in production for metrics and traces. Keep Datadog agents, Datadog APM, Datadog synthetics, Splunk forwarders, and PagerDuty routes unchanged. Add cardinality drop rules for `customer_id`, `session_id`, request IDs, and unapproved dynamic labels. | New stack receives telemetry from at least 9 of 10 services for 48 hours; dropped-label report reviewed by service owners; Datadog panels remain the official incident reference. | Disable Alloy DaemonSet/exporters and restore service env vars to Datadog-only export; rollback is a config revert and DaemonSet scale-down inside 30 minutes. |
| 3 | Build first Grafana service dashboards and SLO burn-rate alerts for `payment-svc`, `checkout-svc`, `catalog-db`, and `edge-lb`. Configure Tempo tail sampling. | Grafana dashboards reproduce current Datadog golden signals within 5% for traffic, error, and latency panels; slow/error traces visible for synthetic incidents. | Re-point runbooks to Datadog dashboards; keep new dashboards read-only. |
| 4 | Introduce Loki for hot incident logs. Send structured logs to Loki while keeping Splunk and Datadog log paths. Begin S3 Parquet archive and Athena audit report prototypes. | Top 20 incident log queries from the last quarter run in Loki at p95 under 8 seconds for a 24-hour window; Athena reproduces 5 audit reports within 2% row count; Splunk remains official for audit. | Remove Loki from runbooks and set Fluent Bit outputs back to Splunk/Datadog-only; keep Splunk and Datadog indexed logs as the incident path inside 30 minutes. |
| 5 | Migrate alert rules for top-tier services into Grafana Alerting and Alertmanager. Add inhibition rules for downstream symptoms from `checkout-svc`, `payment-svc`, `catalog-db`, and `edge-lb`. PagerDuty remains the final page target while Datadog/Splunk monitors shadow the new rules. | 95% of critical/high alert rules have Grafana equivalents; two synthetic cascade tests create one grouped alert each; no Sev1/Sev2 Datadog or Splunk monitor is disabled yet. | Disable Grafana alert routes and return to Datadog/Splunk monitors as PagerDuty sources inside 30 minutes. |
| 6 | Pilot Grafana Cloud IRM with platform and payments on-call. Correlation worker adds service graph, recent deploy, and evidence links. PagerDuty receives duplicate pages silently for comparison, not as primary. | On-call independently triages one synthetic payment-pool incident from IRM in under 5.6 minutes from page to first hypothesis; mobile ack/escalation works for all pilot users; PagerDuty and IRM page sets match for seven days. | Restore PagerDuty as the primary paging route and pause IRM routing inside 30 minutes; IRM remains read-only for timeline review. |
| 7 | Cut over production paging to IRM for all teams. Freeze new Datadog/Splunk alert creation except emergency exceptions. Begin Datadog APM/log decommission for low-criticality services only after one week of dual telemetry. | Seven days with no missed pages; duplicate incident count reduced by at least 60%; incident timelines exported to decision store; on-call reaches first hypothesis under 5.6 minutes in a synthetic cascade. | Switch Alertmanager receiver back to PagerDuty and restore Datadog/Splunk as primary page sources inside 30 minutes; keep IRM read-only and retain PagerDuty schedules for 30 days. |
| 8 | Retire Datadog APM, Datadog indexed logs, Grafana Cloud mirror, and most Splunk hot searches. Keep Splunk contract until non-renewal window and export required audit data within the 100 GB/day cap. Review cost and MTTR targets. | Monthly run-rate forecast is under `$25.2k`; median page-to-first-hypothesis in drills is at least 30% lower than the current 8 minutes, target under 5.6 minutes; top 20 Loki queries and 95% critical/high alert parity remain green. | Re-enable Datadog/Splunk exporters for affected services and pause vendor cancellation inside 30 minutes; no agents, credentials, or Splunk forwarders are deleted until after the rollback window closes. |

## No-Blackout Guarantee

For weeks 1-7, Datadog, Splunk, and PagerDuty remain live and authoritative until the corresponding Grafana/Loki/Tempo/IRM gate passes. All exporter changes are additive first. Destructive actions, such as deleting Datadog agents or disabling Splunk forwarders, occur only after one full week of successful dual-run and outside business hours.
